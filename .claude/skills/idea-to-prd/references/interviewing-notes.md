# Interviewing Notes for idea-to-prd

Use this reference when the input is conversation-heavy, messy, or emotionally framed.

## What to listen for

When someone explains an idea verbally, they often mix together:

- the problem
- the proposed solution
- implementation guesses
- frustrations with current tools
- implied requirements
- fears or constraints

Your job is to separate those.

## Useful extraction lenses

### 1. Problem lens
What pain or inefficiency are they reacting to?

### 2. Outcome lens
What would “better” look like to them?

### 3. Actor lens
Who is affected?
Who uses it?
Who pays?
Who supports it?

### 4. Constraint lens
What sounds like a hard boundary?
Examples:
- privacy
- deadline
- stack
- regulation
- team capability

### 5. Ambiguity lens
What is still fuzzy?
Do not accidentally harden fuzzy ideas into requirements.

## Common mistakes to avoid

- mistaking a suggested implementation for the real requirement
- turning enthusiasm into certainty
- writing vague goals like “make it seamless”
- ignoring unresolved trade-offs
- leaving non-functional concerns buried in paragraphs

## Good open questions

Prefer questions that unblock decisions.

Examples:
- What is the source of truth for this data?
- Which user roles can perform this action?
- What needs to be auditable?
- Is this required for MVP or later?
- What happens in the failure case?

## Principle

Capture intent faithfully, then structure it.
Do not decorate it.
